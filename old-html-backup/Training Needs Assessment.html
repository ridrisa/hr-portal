Training Needs Assessment (TNA)

23. Training Needs Assessment (TNA)

23.0 Purpose

A Training Needs Assessment (TNA) is a systematic process for identifying the gap between current and desired knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) within an organization. A TNA ensures that training investments are targeted, effective, and aligned with strategic business goals, preventing wasteful spending on irrelevant programs.

23.1 The 3-Level Analysis Framework

  1. Organizational Analysis:
  2. Goal: To identify where training is needed within the organization.
  3. Methods: Reviewing business goals, strategic plans, efficiency metrics, and workforce plans.
  4. Question: "Which business units are underperforming against their goals? Where are the biggest skill gaps that are preventing us from achieving our strategic objectives?"
  5. Task/Occupational Analysis:
  6. Goal: To identify what specific skills and knowledge are required to perform a job effectively.
  7. Methods: Analyzing job descriptions, observing employees at work, conducting focus groups with high-performers.
  8. Question: "What are the essential KSAs required for a top-performing 'Data Analyst' in our company? What does excellence look like in this role?"
  9. Individual Analysis:
  10. Goal: To identify which specific employees need training and in what areas.
  11. Methods: Reviewing performance reviews, conducting 360-degree feedback surveys, individual skill assessments, and career development conversations.
  12. Question: "Which of our current data analysts are lacking the required Python skills identified in the task analysis?"
