Designing Salary Structures

21. Designing Salary Structures

21.0 Purpose

A salary structure is a foundational tool for compensation management. It provides a systematic framework for defining the value of different jobs within an organization, ensuring both internal equity (fair pay for similar roles) and external competitiveness (pay that is competitive with the market). This module outlines the step-by-step process for creating a robust, data-driven salary structure.

21.1 The Process Flow

  1. Job Analysis: Understand the duties, responsibilities, and requirements of each job.
  2. Job Evaluation: Use a systematic method (like a point-factor system) to determine the relative worth of jobs within the organization.
  3. Market Benchmarking: Gather salary data from reputable surveys for benchmark jobs.
  4. Pay Policy & Strategy: Define the company's desired market position (e.g., pay at the 50th percentile, 75th percentile).
  5. Create Job Grades: Group jobs of similar internal value into grades.
  6. Define Salary Ranges: For each grade, define a minimum, midpoint, and maximum salary. The midpoint should be aligned with the market data and the company's pay policy.
  7. Implementation & Communication: Place employees into the new structure and communicate the philosophy and mechanics to the organization.
