Writing Effective Job Descriptions

25. Writing Effective Job Descriptions

25.0 The Dual Purpose of a Job Description

An effective job description serves two primary purposes:

  1. An Internal Alignment Tool: It provides a clear, documented understanding of a role's responsibilities, requirements, and reporting structure. It is the foundation for job grading, salary benchmarking, performance management, and career pathing.
  2. An External Marketing Document: It is the source of truth for the job posting. It must accurately and compellingly communicate the role to potential candidates.

25.1 Key Components of a Comprehensive Job Description

  • Job Title & Identification:
  • Official Job Title
  • Department
  • Reports To (Title)
  • Job Grade/Level
  • FLSA Status (for US) / Employment Type (for KSA)
  • Role Summary: A brief, 1-3 sentence paragraph summarizing the core purpose and contribution of the role.
  • Essential Duties & Responsibilities: A bulleted list of the primary, non-negotiable tasks and responsibilities of the job. Use action verbs.
  • Qualifications & Requirements:
  • Education: Minimum required level of education.
  • Experience: Minimum years of relevant experience.
  • Knowledge, Skills, & Abilities (KSAs): A list of the specific competencies and technical skills required (e.g., "Proficiency in SAP SuccessFactors," "Expert knowledge of KSA Labour Law").
  • Working Conditions / Physical Demands: A description of the physical environment and any specific physical requirements of the job (e.g., "Ability to lift up to 20kg," "Frequent travel required").
  • Approval & Version Control:
  • Approved By (Manager & HR)
  • Date Approved
  • Version Number
