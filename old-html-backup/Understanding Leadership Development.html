Understanding Leadership Development Programs

24. Understanding Leadership Development Programs

24.0 Strategic Importance

Leadership Development Programs (LDPs) are a critical component of an organization's succession planning and talent management strategy. The primary goal of an LDP is to build a robust pipeline of future leaders who have the skills, knowledge, and experience to execute the company's strategy and navigate future challenges. Investing in leadership development is a direct investment in the long-term health and sustainability of the organization.

24.1 The 70-20-10 Model for Leadership Development

This is a widely accepted and highly effective framework for structuring leadership development.

  • 70% Experiential Learning (On-the-Job): The most impactful development comes from hands-on experience. This includes:
  • Stretch Assignments: Giving high-potential employees challenging projects that are outside of their current comfort zone.
  • Cross-Functional Rotations: Moving employees through different departments to give them a holistic view of the business.
  • Leading a Task Force or Project Team.
  • 20% Social Learning (From Others): Learning through observation, feedback, and relationships. This includes:
  • Mentoring: Pairing a high-potential employee with a senior leader.
  • Coaching: Providing access to a professional executive coach.
  • Peer Learning Circles: Small groups of peers who meet regularly to discuss challenges and share best practices.
  • 10% Formal Learning (Coursework): Structured, formal training programs. This includes:
  • University Executive Education Programs.
  • Internal workshops on topics like strategic thinking, financial acumen, or leading change.
  • **E-learning modules on core leadership competencies.
