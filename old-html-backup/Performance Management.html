18. Performance Management

18.0 The Evolution: From Appraisal to Enablement

Modern performance management has undergone a fundamental shift. The outdated model of a single, backward-looking annual appraisal is being replaced by a continuous, forward-looking process focused on performance enablement. The new paradigm de-emphasizes rating and ranking and instead prioritizes clear goal setting, regular feedback, ongoing coaching, and data-driven development. The objective is no longer simply to judge past performance but to actively improve future performance and accelerate employee growth.

  1. Plan: At the beginning of each quarter, employees and managers collaborate to set clear, ambitious goals using a framework like OKRs.
  2. Monitor: Throughout the quarter, progress is monitored through regular, lightweight check-ins (weekly or bi-weekly). This is where real-time feedback and coaching happen.
  3. Develop: These check-ins help identify skill gaps or roadblocks in real-time, triggering just-in-time development or support.
  4. Review: At the end of the quarter, a more formal conversation takes place to review progress against goals, summarize key achievements, and set the stage for the next quarter.
  5. Reward: On an annual or semi-annual basis, the cumulative output of the quarterly reviews informs compensation, promotion, and recognition decisions.
